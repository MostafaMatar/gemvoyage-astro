# Best Times to Visit Thailand — Short Guide

TL;DR: November–February (cool season) for best overall weather; March–May is hot but lively (Songkran); May–Oct (rainy season) has lower prices and lush scenery—pick coast depending on month (Andaman vs Gulf).

## Why timing matters
Thailand’s weather varies between coasts. The Andaman coast (Phuket, Krabi) and Gulf islands (Koh Samui) have different monsoon schedules. Festivals like Songkran (Apr) and Loy Krathong (Nov) are major draws.

## Best months by interest
- Beach & diving (Andaman): Nov–Apr
- Gulf islands (Koh Samui): Jan–Sep (drier when Andaman is wet)
- Festivals: Apr (Songkran), Nov (Loy Krathong)
- Budget travel: May–Oct (rainy season discounts)

## Regional quick notes
- Bangkok: Nov–Feb best for city exploring.
- Chiang Mai: Nov–Feb cool, Feb for flower season; avoid burning season (Mar–Apr).
- Phuket/Krabi (Andaman): Nov–Apr prime season.
- Koh Samui & Gulf: Jan–Sep best window.

## 5-day sample itinerary (family-friendly)
Day 1: Bangkok — Grand Palace, boat ride and kid-friendly markets.  
Day 2: Fly to Chiang Mai — temples and ethical elephant sanctuary visit.  
Day 3: Night bazaar and easy family trekking.  
Day 4: Fly to Phuket or Koh Samui — beach day.  
Day 5: Relax and depart.

## Packing checklist
- Cool season: light layers and insect repellent.
- Hot season: breathable clothes, sun protection, refillable water bottle.
- Rainy season: quick-dry clothing, waterproof bag, sandals that dry fast.

## Budget & booking tips
- Peak (Dec–Jan): book flights and hotels early.
- Rainy season: best bargains but expect short heavy showers and possible transport delays.

## Safety & health tips
- Use bottled water if unsure, use sunscreen and mosquito repellent.
- Respect temple dress codes and local customs.

## Quick FAQ
Q: When is the best time for diving?  
A: Andaman coast: Nov–Apr; Gulf islands: Mar–Sep (varies by site).  
Q: Is Songkran family-friendly?  
A: Yes—fun and playful, but watch for safety in big crowds and slippery streets.
