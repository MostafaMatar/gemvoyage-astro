# Best Times to Visit Costa Rica — Rainforest, Volcanoes & Beaches

TL;DR: Dry season (Dec–Apr) is best for beaches and cloudforest hikes; green/wet season (May–Nov) brings lush scenery, fewer tourists and lower prices—best for surfers and wildlife photographers.

## Why timing matters
Costa Rica’s microclimates mean weather varies by coast and elevation. Pacific side often has a pronounced dry season; Caribbean coast can be wetter but has its own drier spells. National park access, river levels and species sightings are seasonal.

## Best months by interest
- Pacific beaches & Nicoya Peninsula: Dec–Apr for sunshine and calm seas.  
- Caribbean coast (Tortuguero, Puerto Viejo): Sep–Nov and Feb–Apr can be good, depending on microclimate.  
- Cloud forests & Monteverde: Dec–Mar and Sep–Oct—trail conditions better in dry months.  
- Surfing: May–Nov on the Pacific; Caribbean swells vary.

## Regional quick notes
- Manuel Antonio & Nicoya: family beaches bloom in dry season; watch for shoulder months for fewer crowds.  
- Tortuguero: turtle nesting peaks vary—check specific species and local windows.  
- Arenal/La Fortuna: year-round volcano views; dry months easier for hikes.

## 5-day sample itinerary (family-friendly)
Day 1: San José — cultural museums and child-friendly parks.  
Day 2: Transfer to Arenal — hot springs and easy trails.  
Day 3: Zipline or hanging bridges (age-appropriate options).  
Day 4: Drive to Pacific coast (Manuel Antonio or Nicoya) — beach and wildlife.  
Day 5: Relaxed beach morning and return to San José.

## Packing checklist
- Quick-dry clothing, waterproof jacket for rain season, insect repellent, sturdy hiking shoes and reef-safe sunscreen.

## Budget & booking tips
- Peak dry-season months are busiest—book eco-lodges and guided tours in advance. Rainy season has lower rates and dramatic wildlife viewing.

## Safety & family tips
- Follow wildlife viewing rules and avoid feeding animals. For river and ocean activities, use licensed operators and lifejackets for kids.

## Quick FAQ
Q: When is turtle nesting season?  
A: It depends on species and beach—Tortuguero green turtles nest roughly Jul–Oct; leatherbacks have different peaks.  
Q: Is Costa Rica good for families?  
A: Absolutely—lots of guided, safe nature activities and family-friendly eco-lodges.
