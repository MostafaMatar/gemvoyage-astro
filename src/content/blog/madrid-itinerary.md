# Madrid in 3 or 5 Days — Practical Itineraries and Travel Tips

Madrid combines grand museums, lively plazas, and a thriving food scene. Use 3 days for highlights and 5 days to add day trips and deeper cultural dives.

## 3-Day Madrid Itinerary (Highlights)
Day 1 — Prado, Retiro Park, and tapas in La Latina.  
Day 2 — Royal Palace, Almudena Cathedral, and Gran Vía shopping.  
Day 3 — Reina Sofía (Guernica), Mercado de San Miguel, and evening flamenco.

## 5-Day Madrid Itinerary (Deeper exploration)
Add: day trip to Toledo or Segovia, Thyssen-Bornemisza visit, Malasaña and Chueca neighborhoods, and a market/cooking experience.

## Variations
For families — shorter museum visits, Retiro rowboats, and Faunia or Madrid Zoo.  
For young explorers — nightlife in Malasaña, rooftop bars, and street-food markets.

## Short FAQ
Q: Best way to move around? A: Metro and walking; day passes are useful.  
Q: How many days? A: 3 for essentials; 5 for a relaxed trip plus a day trip.

## Summary
Madrid rewards both quick cultural stops and a relaxed itinerary with day trips. Book major museums ahead and enjoy tapas for efficient dining.
