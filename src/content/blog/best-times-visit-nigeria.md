# Best Times to Visit Nigeria — Practical Guide for Families & Young Travelers

TL;DR: The best months are November–March (dry season) for city exploring, coastal beaches and national parks. Rainy season (Apr–Oct) brings lush landscapes but can complicate travel in rural areas; shoulder months offer trade-offs between price and weather.

## Why timing matters
Nigeria’s climate varies from the humid tropical south (Lagos, the Niger Delta) to the drier savannah in the north (Sokoto, Kano). Road conditions and domestic flight reliability can be affected by heavy rains. Festivals and cultural events are season-driven, and planning around them gives richer experiences.

## Best months by interest
- Lagos & coastal beaches: Nov–Mar for dry, sunny days and calm seas.  
- Abuja & central plateau: Nov–Feb for comfortable daytime temps.  
- Northern cultural tours (Kano, Sokoto): Nov–Feb avoid extreme heat.  
- Wildlife & national parks (Yankari, Cross River): Nov–Mar for easier game drives and trail walking.

## Regional quick notes
- Lagos: lively year-round—dry months best for beach and outdoor nightlife.  
- Abuja: planned city with good access to nearby hills and parks—cooler evenings in dry season.  
- Calabar & Cross River: rich culture and festivals (e.g., Calabar Carnival in Dec) but check local dates.  
- Northern region: distinct seasons—avoid extreme heat in peak summer months.

## 5-day sample itinerary (family-friendly)
Day 1: Arrive Lagos — Lekki conservation centre visit and beach time at Lekki/Vi.  
Day 2: Explore Lagos islands, National Museum and kid-friendly science/arts spaces.  
Day 3: Fly to Abuja — visit Millennium Park, National Mosque and Sunday markets.  
Day 4: Day trip to Gurara Falls or nearby nature reserve for family picnic.  
Day 5: Fly to Calabar (seasonal) or return via Lagos for departure.

## Packing checklist
- Dry season: light layers, sun protection, insect repellent, comfortable walking shoes.  
- Rainy season: waterproof jacket, quick-dry clothes, reliable footwear for muddy trails.  
- Essentials: travel adapter, bottled water options and basic first-aid.

## Budget & booking tips
- Peak travel months (Dec–Jan) align with holidays and events—book flights and hotels early.  
- Shoulder months provide better value; internal flights are efficient but book in advance during peak.

## Safety & family tips
- Use reputable transfers and hotel-arranged transport; public roads can be busy and signage inconsistent.  
- Drink bottled water and follow local food safety advice for families with small children.  
- Respect local customs and dress modestly in conservative areas.

## Quick FAQ
Q: When is the best time to visit Lagos beaches?  
A: November–March for dryer, sunnier days.  
Q: Are Nigeria's festivals worth timing a trip for?  
A: Yes—events like Calabar Carnival and regional cultural festivals provide unforgettable cultural experiences.
