# Best Times to Visit Oman — Mountains, Desert & Coast

TL;DR: October–April offers cool, dry weather—perfect for mountain treks, desert camping and seaside relaxation. Summers are very hot, especially inland, but the coast has some humidity-moderated spots.

## Why timing matters
Oman’s diverse geography—from Hajar Mountains to the Empty Quarter and long coastline—means each region has its best season. Mountain trails and wadis become pleasant in cooler months while the frankincense coast and Musandam fjords have niche windows.

## Best months by interest
- Muscat & coast: Oct–Apr for comfortable sightseeing.  
- Jebel Akhdar & Hajar Mountains: Oct–Apr for cooler hiking temps.  
- Wahiba Sands desert: Oct–Apr for safe, pleasant camping nights.  
- Dhofar (Salalah monsoon): Jun–Sep brings green season—unique cultural landscape but some rain.

## Regional quick notes
- Muscat: best enjoyed in winter for outdoor souks and corniche walks.  
- Salalah: visit during Khareef (monsoon) for lush landscapes if you want a different Oman experience.  
- Offshore diving: year-round in some spots, but check local visibility windows.

## 5-day sample itinerary (family-friendly)
Day 1: Muscat — Grand Mosque, Mutrah Souq and waterfront.  
Day 2: Drive to Nizwa — fort and local market experience.  
Day 3: Jebel Akhdar day hikes and terrace villages.  
Day 4: Wahiba Sands desert camp with guided activities.  
Day 5: Coastal day at Ras Al Jinz (turtle viewing) or Salalah (seasonal).

## Packing checklist
- Layers, sun protection, sturdy shoes for mountain walking, and warm layer for desert nights.

## Budget & booking tips
- Peak season is Oct–Apr—book desert camps and popular mountain hotels early. Khareef season in Salalah sees domestic crowds—plan ahead if visiting then.

## Safety & family tips
- Respect conservative dress in towns and religious sites. Use guided operators for desert and mountain excursions and ensure vehicles are suitable for rough roads.

## Quick FAQ
Q: When is Khareef in Salalah?  
A: Jun–Sep—the monsoon green season unique in Arabia.  
Q: Is Oman family-friendly?  
A: Yes—calm beaches, cultural sites and safe desert camps suitable for older children.
