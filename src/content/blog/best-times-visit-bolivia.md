# Best Times to Visit Bolivia — Highlands, Salt Flats & Jungle

TL;DR: April–October (dry season) is the most practical window—clear skies for Uyuni, solid trekking in the highlands and easier jungle access. The wet season (Nov–Mar) can close roads and flood lowland areas but creates dramatic green Amazon scenery.

## Why timing matters
Bolivia’s geography ranges from high Andes altiplano to Amazon lowlands. Altitude, seasonal rainfall and transport reliability shape experience—clear, dry months mean better road access, clearer photos at Uyuni and safer hiking. Rainy months make some remote routes impassable and complicate internal flights.

## Best months by interest
- Uyuni Salt Flats: May–Oct for dry, mirror-free expanses (mirror effect appears briefly in wet months but is dependent on shallow water).  
- La Paz & Lake Titicaca: Apr–Oct for crisp skies and easier acclimatization.  
- Amazon (Rurrenabaque, Madidi): Jul–Nov (lower water for trails), but wet season offers flooded forest boat access.  
- Trekking (Cordillera Real, Sajama): Apr–Oct for stable trails and mountain views.

## Regional quick notes
- La Paz: high altitude city—allow time to acclimatize; best in shoulder seasons.  
- Salar de Uyuni: early dry-season mornings offer crisp horizons; consider multi-day salt tours from Uyuni town.  
- Southern altiplano (Potosí/Sucre): clear, dry months ideal for colonial towns and mining visits.  
- Amazonian east: river levels define whether boat or foot travel is preferable—consult operators.

## 5-day sample itinerary (family-friendly)
Day 1: Arrive La Paz — gentle city walk, cable car views and acclimatization.  
Day 2: Day trip to Moon Valley and local markets.  
Day 3: Fly to Uyuni — salt flat sunset and simple salt-hotel stay.  
Day 4: Full salt-flat day: Incahuasi, red lagoons and flamingos (dry season best).  
Day 5: Return to Uyuni and fly to Sucre or La Paz for onward travel.

## Packing checklist
- High-altitude layers, sun protection, sunglasses, sturdy footwear, water purification tablets for remote areas, and insect repellent for Amazon trips.

## Budget & booking tips
- Dry season sees higher prices around Uyuni—book tours 1–2 months ahead. Low-season deals exist but come with access risk. Domestic flights to Uyuni and Rurrenabaque can be limited; reserve early.

## Safety & family tips
- Acclimatize slowly; avoid strenuous activity on arrival. Use licensed guides for salt-flat and jungle trips. Stay hydrated and carry altitude medication if needed.

## Quick FAQ
Q: When is the mirror effect on Uyuni?  
A: Typically during the wet season (Nov–Mar) after rains; travel windows vary yearly.  
Q: Is Bolivia family-friendly?  
A: Yes—with planning: shorter itineraries and altitude-aware pacing make it enjoyable for older kids.
