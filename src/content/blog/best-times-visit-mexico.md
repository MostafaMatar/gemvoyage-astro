# Best Times to Visit Mexico — Casual Guide

TL;DR: Nov–Apr is generally the best time for most regions (dry season). May–Oct is warmer and rainy—great for bargains but watch hurricane season on Caribbean coast (Aug–Oct). Día de los Muertos (late Oct–Nov) is a cultural highlight.

## Why timing matters
Mexico's climate ranges from desert to tropical. Coastal hurricane risk, rainy season timing and festival periods influence both safety and experience. Pick north, central or coastal windows based on activities.

## Best months by interest
- Mexico City & central highlands: Mar–May, Sep–Nov for mild weather and festivals.
- Caribbean coast (Cancún, Riviera Maya): Nov–Apr to avoid hurricanes.
- Pacific coast (Puerto Vallarta, Oaxaca coast): Nov–May for dry season.
- Baja California surfing & whale watching: Dec–Apr.

## Regional quick notes
- Yucatán (Chichén Itzá, Tulum): best Nov–Apr.
- Oaxaca & cultural regions: festival season around late Oct–Nov and summer craft fairs.
- Copper Canyon & northern deserts: spring and fall for comfortable temps.

## 5-day sample itinerary (family-friendly)
Day 1: Mexico City — kid-friendly museums, Chapultepec Park.  
Day 2: Teotihuacan day trip and local food market.  
Day 3: Fly to Cancún or Playa del Carmen — beach afternoon.  
Day 4: Cenote swim and eco-park visit.  
Day 5: Relaxed beach day or ferry to Cozumel for snorkeling.

## Packing checklist
- Lightweight clothing for coasts, breathable layers for Mexico City evenings.
- Reef-safe sunscreen, insect repellent, reusable water bottle with filter if preferred.

## Budget & booking tips
- Peak rates around Christmas, Easter and spring break—book months ahead.
- Shoulder seasons offer deals but check hurricane forecasts for Caribbean coast.

## Safety & family tips
- Use registered taxis or apps in cities; avoid isolated areas after dark.
- Stick to bottled water outside major hotels, especially for young children.
- Respect local customs and be cautious with street food choices for kids.

## Quick FAQ
Q: When is whale-watching season?  
A: Baja California: Dec–Apr (gray whale migration).  
Q: Is it safe to visit Yucatán in summer?  
A: You can, but be mindful of heat and hurricane season; plan travel insurance.
