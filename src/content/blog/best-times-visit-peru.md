# Best Times to Visit Peru — Highlands, Coast and Jungle

TL;DR: May–September is Peru’s dry season—best for trekking to Machu Picchu and clear Andean skies. Amazon low-water months (Jul–Nov) make river travel easier, while Lima’s winter (May–Sep) is cooler and misty but mild.

## Why timing matters
Peru’s elevations create distinct weather: Andean trails close in wet months and cloud cover affects visibility at high sites. The Amazon has differing river levels impacting access and wildlife viewing style. Festivals (Inti Raymi in June) are key cultural draws.

## Best months by interest
- Machu Picchu & Inca Trail: May–Sep for dry trails and views (Apr and Oct are shoulder seasons).
- Amazon (Iquitos, Manu): Jul–Nov for lower water levels and easier jungle walks; wet season Nov–Apr gives more flooded landscapes and canoe access.
- Lima & Peruvian coast: Dec–Mar warmer and sunnier; winter highs are cool and grey.

## Regional quick notes
- Cusco: high-altitude city—plan for thin air and pack warm layers for nights.
- Sacred Valley: spring and autumn offer excellent light for photography and fewer crowds.
- Arequipa & Colca Canyon: dry season best for condor sightings and hikes.

## 5-day sample itinerary (family-friendly)
Day 1: Lima — Miraflores parks, kid-friendly museums and coastal walk.  
Day 2: Fly to Cusco — acclimatize, gentle city walk and local markets.  
Day 3: Sacred Valley — Pisac market and Ollantaytambo ruins.  
Day 4: Machu Picchu day visit by train (family-friendly option) or multi-day trek.  
Day 5: Return to Cusco and fly home.

## Packing checklist
- High-altitude layers, sun protection, comfortable hiking shoes and motion-sickness remedies for train/bus legs.

## Budget & booking tips
- Machu Picchu permits and popular tourist trains sell out—book months ahead for high season. Shoulder months can save money with good weather.

## Safety & family tips
- Acclimatize in Cusco before strenuous hikes. Use certified guides for treks and jungle excursions.
- Drink bottled water in many regions and follow local health advice for Amazon areas.

## Quick FAQ
Q: When is Inti Raymi?  
A: June 24 (Cusco) — rich cultural celebration but busy and requires advance planning.  
Q: Best time for Amazon jungle walks?  
A: Jul–Nov for lower water levels and more comfortable trekking.
