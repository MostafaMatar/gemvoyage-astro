# Palermo in 3 or 5 Days — Practical Itineraries and Travel Tips

Palermo is a lively Sicilian city where Arab-Norman architecture mixes with street markets and intense coastal flavors. Expect busy markets, layered history, and short trips to nearby beaches and towns.

## Quick tips
- Best months: Apr–Jun and Sep–Oct for pleasant seaside weather.
- Transport: Walkable center; buses for outlying sites.  
- Try: street food (arancini, panelle) and visit markets early.

## 3-Day Palermo Itinerary (Highlights)
Day 1 — Teatro Massimo, Quattro Canti, and Palermo Cathedral.  
Day 2 — Capo & Ballarò markets, Norman Palace (Palazzo dei Normanni) and Cappella Palatina.  
Day 3 — Monreale Cathedral visit and afternoon at Mondello beach.

## 5-Day Palermo Itinerary (Deeper exploration)
Add: day trips to Cefalù, the Zisa and Palazzo Abatellis museums, and relaxed time on the northwest coast.

## Variations: Family-Friendly vs. Young Explorer
Families — shorter market visits, beach time in Mondello, and the botanical gardens.  
Young explorers — nightlife in Kalsa, street food crawls, and coastal hikes.

## Short FAQ
Q: Is Palermo safe for tourists?  
A: Yes—usual urban caution; keep an eye on belongings in busy markets.  
Q: How to reach Monreale?  
A: Short bus or taxi ride from Palermo central area.

## Summary
Palermo rewards food-focused and historical explorations. A 3-day stay covers the core; 5 days allow coastal day trips and deeper museum visits.
