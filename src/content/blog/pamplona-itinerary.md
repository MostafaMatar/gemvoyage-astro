# Pamplona in 3 or 5 Days — Practical Itineraries and Travel Tips

Pamplona (home to Club Atlético Osasuna) is a compact, historic city in Navarra with leafy promenades, fortified citadels and strong regional cuisine. It’s peaceful outside festival season and offers easy access to countryside hikes and wine routes.

## 3-Day Pamplona Itinerary (Highlights)
Day 1 — Old Town and Plaza del Castillo.  
Day 2 — Walk the City Walls, visit the Citadel and local museums.  
Day 3 — Day trip into Navarra: Roncesvalles or wine routes.

## 5-Day Pamplona Itinerary (Deeper exploration)
Add: hiking in the Irati Forest, Bardenas Reales desert visit, and small villages of Navarra.

## Variations
For families — park time along the Arga River and gentle countryside walks.  
For young explorers — tapas tours and hiking in nearby natural parks.

## Short FAQ
Q: Is Pamplona only about San Fermín? A: No — festivals are famous, but the city offers year-round culture and nature.  
Q: How to reach Pamplona? A: Trains/buses from Madrid or Bilbao; regional flights serve Pamplona Airport.

## Summary
Pamplona balances historic charm and outdoor access. A 3-day visit hits the city; 5 days let you explore Navarra’s varied landscapes.
