# Córdoba in 3 or 5 Days — Practical Itineraries and Travel Tips

Córdoba is a jewel of Andalusia: the Mezquita’s layered history, whitewashed patios and a compact old quarter make it perfect for slow exploration.

## 3-Day Córdoba Itinerary (Highlights)
Day 1 — Mezquita, Alcázar of the Christian Monarchs, and Roman Bridge.  
Day 2 — Jewish Quarter, Synagogue, and Patio walking.  
Day 3 — Day trip to Medina Azahara or local olive groves.

## 5-Day Córdoba Itinerary (Deeper exploration)
Add: visits to Sierra Morena, local flamenco shows, and culinary experiences focusing on salmorejo and regional tapas.

## Variations
For families — guided short tours and garden breaks; avoid the hottest midday sun.  
For young explorers — late tapas crawls and patio photography.

## Short FAQ
Q: Best time to visit? A: Spring for the Patio Festival (May) and milder weather.  
Q: Is the Mezquita crowded? A: It can be; arrive early or late afternoon.

## Summary
Córdoba’s compact heritage rewards a 3-day visit, while 5 days allow relaxed day trips and immersion in Andalusian culture.
