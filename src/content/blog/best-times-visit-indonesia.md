# Best Times to Visit Indonesia — Short Practical Guide

TL;DR: Dry season (Apr–Oct) is best for most islands—Bali, Lombok, Komodo and Java. Wet season (Nov–Mar) can have heavy rains but also fewer tourists and lower prices. Choose islands by activity and tolerance for showers.

## Why timing matters
Indonesia spans many islands with varied microclimates. Monsoon-driven rains influence beach time and boat safety; festivals like Nyepi (Bali) affect local life and openings.

## Best months by interest
- Bali & Lombok: Apr–Oct dry season for beaches and trekking.
- Komodo & Flores: Apr–Oct for boat trips and diving.
- Sumatra & Borneo (wildlife): May–Sep for clearer trails and river safaris.
- Java cultural sites (Borobudur, Prambanan): Apr–Oct for dry weather.

## Regional quick notes
- Bali: dry season ideal for beaches and outdoor activities; Nyepi (silent day) usually in March so plan accordingly.
- Komodo: best Apr–Oct for clear water and dragon trekking.
- Raja Ampat (Papua): different seasonality—check local windows for diving.

## 5-day sample itinerary (family-friendly)
Day 1: Denpasar/Bali — beach and family-friendly resorts.  
Day 2: Ubud — monkey forest, rice terraces and cultural dance.  
Day 3: Day trip to Nusa Lembongan for snorkeling.  
Day 4: Fly to Labuan Bajo (Komodo) — start island-hopping or diving.  
Day 5: Komodo National Park trip or local cultural day.

## Packing checklist
- Reef-safe sunscreen, lightweight rain jacket for sudden showers, snorkeling gear if you have it.

## Budget & booking tips
- Peak dry-season months fill hotels—book flights and boats ahead. Wet season can deliver big discounts but check ferry reliability.

## Safety & health tips
- Use licensed tour operators for Komodo and remote islands; carry basic first-aid and ensure travel insurance covers boating.

## Quick FAQ
Q: When is Bali busiest?  
A: July–Aug and Dec–Jan holiday periods.  
Q: Can you dive year-round in Indonesia?  
A: Many dive sites best in dry season Apr–Oct; check local conditions for remote reefs.
