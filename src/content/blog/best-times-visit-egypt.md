# Best Times to Visit Egypt — Practical Guide

TL;DR: Visit in winter (Dec–Feb) for comfortable sightseeing and Nile cruises; October–November and Feb–April are excellent shoulder months. Avoid peak summer unless you plan beach time or want empty archaeological sites and low prices.

## Why timing matters
Egypt’s climate ranges from Mediterranean on the coast to blazing desert inland. Heat affects how much you’ll enjoy walking between ruins and lines at the Pyramids. Timing also affects cruise schedules and festival dates.

## Best months by activity
- Sightseeing (Cairo, Luxor, Aswan): Nov–Feb (cool days)
- Nile cruises: Oct–Apr (comfortable mornings/evenings)
- Red Sea diving & beaches: Mar–May and Sep–Nov
- Budget travel & empty sites: Jun–Aug (extreme heat)

## Practical regional notes
- Cairo/Giza: avoid midday in summer; early morning visits recommended.
- Luxor/Aswan: summers can exceed 40°C; plan early starts.
- Red Sea (Hurghada, Sharm): year-round diving; spring/fall best for comfortable water temps.
- Alexandria: milder summers because of Mediterranean influence.

## 5-day sample itinerary (family-friendly)
Day 1: Cairo — Pyramids, Sphinx, short papyrus workshop for kids.  
Day 2: Cairo museum, flight to Luxor late afternoon.  
Day 3: Luxor East/West Bank highlights (Valley of the Kings).  
Day 4: Start/continue Nile cruise or day trip to Edfu/Kom Ombo.  
Day 5: Aswan — Philae and felucca ride, fly back.

## Packing checklist & health
- Lightweight breathable clothes, hat, sunscreen, reusable water bottle.
- Modest clothes for religious sites (shoulders/knees covered).
- Meds, oral rehydration salts; travel insurance recommended.

## Budget & booking tips
- Winter popular: book Nile cruises and guided tours 2–3 months in advance.
- Summer deals: significant discounts on hotels and some tours.
- Local taxis & tours: agree price or use metered services/official counters.

## Safety & cultural tips
- Respect local customs, especially during Ramadan.
- Avoid drinking tap water—use bottled for drinking and brushing.
- Stay hydrated and plan morning/late-afternoon site visits in hot months.

## Quick FAQ
Q: Is Egypt good for families?  
A: Yes—kids love tombs, camel rides and Nile felucca trips, but avoid hottest months for small children.  
Q: When are Nile cruises best?  
A: Oct–Apr for comfortable temperatures and sightseeing.
