# Alexandria in 3 or 5 Days — Practical Itineraries and Travel Tips

Alexandria blends Hellenistic heritage and Mediterranean charm: seaside promenades, historic libraries, and Ottoman-era architecture. It’s calmer than Cairo and ideal for relaxed cultural exploration.

Quick tips
- Best months: Oct–Apr for milder coastal weather.
- Transport: Taxis and ride-hailing apps; consider a local guide for archaeological sites.
- Currency: Egyptian Pound (EGP); carry small change for local vendors.

## 3-Day Alexandria Itinerary (Highlights)

Day 1 — Bibliotheca & Corniche
- Morning: Bibliotheca Alexandrina and its museums.
- Afternoon: Walk the Corniche to enjoy sea views and local cafés.
- Evening: Seafood dinner by the harbor.

Day 2 — Citadel & Roman ruins
- Morning: Citadel of Qaitbay on the old lighthouse site.
- Afternoon: Kom El Shoqafa catacombs and Roman amphitheatre.
- Evening: Stroll through the Al-Montazah gardens (if time).

Day 3 — Montazah Palace & neighborhoods
- Morning: Montazah Palace and its public gardens.
- Afternoon: Graeco-Roman Museum and local markets.
- Evening: Sunset on the Corniche.

## 5-Day Alexandria Itinerary (Deeper exploration)

Day 1 — Bibliotheca & local museums  
Day 2 — Citadel and Roman Alexandria sites  
Day 3 — Day trip: Rosetta (Rashid) or nearby Nile Delta towns  
Day 4 — Montazah and Alexandria’s palatial coast  
Day 5 — Markets, contemporary galleries, and relaxed seaside time

Why 5 days: Allows day trips and more time for museums and seaside relaxation.

## Variations: Family-Friendly vs. Young Explorer

For families
- Focus on easy coastal walks, Montazah gardens, and short museum visits.
- Choose beachfront hotels for calmer evenings.

For young explorers
- Seek out less-visited archaeological pockets, local cafés, and fish markets; try street photography along the Corniche.

## Short FAQ

Q: How many days for Alexandria?  
A: 2–3 days for highlights; 5 days if adding day trips and relaxed seaside time.

Q: Is the Citadel worth visiting?  
A: Yes — it's built on the site of the ancient lighthouse and offers great views.

## Summary
Alexandria is a quieter coastal complement to Cairo’s intensity. A few days let you enjoy its museums, Roman ruins, and Mediterranean atmosphere, while 5 days add day trips and leisurely seaside afternoons.
