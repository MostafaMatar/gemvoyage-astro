# Florence in 3 or 5 Days — Practical Itineraries and Travel Tips

Florence is the cradle of the Renaissance: compact, walkable and rich with museums, palaces, and Tuscan cuisine. Book top museums in advance.

## Quick tips
- Best months: Apr–Jun and Sep–Oct.  
- Book Uffizi and Accademia tickets online to skip lines.

## 3-Day Florence Itinerary (Highlights)
Day 1 — Duomo, Baptistery, and climb Brunelleschi's dome.  
Day 2 — Uffizi Gallery and Ponte Vecchio; afternoon in Oltrarno workshops.  
Day 3 — Accademia (David), Mercato Centrale and evening sunset at Piazzale Michelangelo.

## 5-Day Florence Itinerary (Deeper exploration)
Add: day trips to Siena, San Gimignano, and Chianti wine tasting; deeper time in Boboli Gardens and Palazzo Pitti.

## Variations
Families — interactive museum trails and gelato workshops; young explorers — artisan studios and local craft beer.

## Short FAQ
Q: Is one day enough for Florence?  
A: One day is rushed; 3 days allow meaningful museum visits and a Tuscan side trip.

## Summary
Florence rewards art-focused itineraries and slow Tuscan days. Use 3 days for essentials; 5 days for countryside and wine regions.
