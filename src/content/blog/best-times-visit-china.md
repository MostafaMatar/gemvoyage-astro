# Best Times to Visit China — Practical Guide

TL;DR: Spring (Apr–May) and autumn (Sep–Oct) are the best windows for comfortable weather and clear skies. Avoid Golden Week (first week of Oct) and major national holidays if you dislike crowds.

## Why timing matters
China spans temperate to subtropical zones. Air quality, monsoon rains and holidays strongly affect travel comfort. Festival timing (Chinese New Year, Mid-Autumn) drives domestic travel and overcrowding at attractions.

## Best months by interest
- Cities (Beijing, Shanghai): Apr–May, Sep–Oct for mild weather.
- Southern China (Guangzhou, Guilin): Oct–Dec avoids summer humidity and typhoons.
- Tibet/Plateau travel: May–Sep (trekking season).
- Best beach weather (Hainan): Nov–Mar.

## Regional quick notes
- Beijing/Forbidden City: spring & autumn—cold winter, hot humid summer.
- Guilin & Yangshuo: late autumn for clear river views.
- Sichuan (Chengdu): year-round; spring for blossoms and panda viewing.
- Yunnan: mild year-round; best in spring/early autumn.

## 5-day sample itinerary (family-friendly)
Day 1: Beijing — Forbidden City, Temple of Heaven, hutong stroll.  
Day 2: Great Wall day trip (mutianyu family-friendly sections).  
Day 3: Fly to Xi’an — Terracotta Army afternoon.  
Day 4: High-speed train to Shanghai — Bund, science museum for kids.  
Day 5: Optional day trip to water town or Disneyland.

## Packing checklist
- Layers for spring/autumn, waterproofs for monsoon season, warm coat for northern winter.
- Comfortable shoes, reusable water bottle, portable air purifier if sensitive to AQI.

## Budget & booking tips
- Book trains and intercity flights early around national holidays.
- Travel in shoulder seasons for lower prices and fewer crowds.

## Safety & cultural tips
- Carry ID (passport) as many places request it for tickets.
- Be mindful of local food hygiene; bottled water recommended.
- Respect local customs and crowded-site etiquette.

## Quick FAQ
Q: When is Chinese New Year?  
A: Varies (Jan–Feb) — expect heavy domestic travel and closed attractions.  
Q: Can I visit Tibet year-round?  
A: No—best May–Sep for access and clearer skies.
