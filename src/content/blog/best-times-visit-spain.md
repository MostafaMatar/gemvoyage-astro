# Best Times to Visit Spain — Straightforward Tips

TL;DR: April–June and Sept–Oct for festivals, comfortable weather and fewer crowds. July–Aug beaches are lively but very hot in inland Spain; winter is perfect for Canary Islands escapes.

## Why timing matters
Spain’s cultural calendar (Semana Santa, La Tomatina, San Fermín) and regional climates (Andalusia heat vs. northern rain) make dates important. Evening culture and late dining mean longer summer nights but also heat.

## Best months by interest
- Festivals & spring: March–May (La Fallas, Semana Santa)
- Beaches & islands: June–Sept (Canary Islands year-round)
- Autumn food & wine: Sept–Oct
- Winter skiing: Dec–Mar (Pyrenees, Sierra Nevada)

## Regional quick notes
- Andalusia (Seville, Córdoba): spring is amazing; summer may be extreme.
- Basque Country: best June–Sept for cooler climate and culinary festivals.
- Canary Islands: year-round mild weather—best winter escape.

## 5-day sample itinerary (family-friendly)
Day 1: Barcelona — Gaudí sites and beach time.  
Day 2: Train to Valencia — paella and City of Arts.  
Day 3: Head to Madrid — parks and museums.  
Day 4: Day trip to Toledo or Segovia.  
Day 5: Fly to southern coast or Canary Islands for beach relaxation.

## Packing checklist
- Spring/Autumn: layers and light rain jacket.
- Summer: sun protection and breathable clothes.
- Winter (north): warm layers; Canary Islands—light clothing.

## Budget & booking tips
- Semana Santa and major festivals: book well in advance.
- Shoulder months: best balance of cost and comfort.

## Quick FAQ
Q: When is La Tomatina?  
A: Last Wednesday of August (Buñol)—book ahead if attending.  
Q: Best time for Canary Islands?  
A: Year-round; winter for warm escape from European cold.
