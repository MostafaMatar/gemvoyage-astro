# Paris in 3 or 5 Days — Practical Itineraries and Travel Tips

Paris is elegant, dense with art, cafés, and history. From grand museums like the Louvre to neighborhood charm in Le Marais and Montmartre, the city rewards strolls and timed visits. This guide gives compact and extended itineraries to cover highlights and deeper experiences.

Quick tips
- Best months: Apr–Jun and Sep–Oct for mild weather and fewer crowds.
- Transport: Metro is efficient; buy a carnet for short stays.
- Book major museums and Eiffel Tower tickets in advance.

## 3-Day Paris Itinerary (Highlights)

Day 1 — Central classics
- Morning: Louvre (choose priority exhibits), then walk to Île de la Cité to see Notre-Dame exterior and Sainte-Chapelle.
- Afternoon: Tuileries and Place de la Concorde.
- Evening: Seine cruise at dusk.

Day 2 — Eiffel Tower & Musée d'Orsay
- Morning: Eiffel Tower and Champ de Mars (book elevator tickets).
- Afternoon: Musée d'Orsay for Impressionist masterpieces.
- Evening: Dinner in Saint-Germain-des-Prés.

Day 3 — Montmartre & Le Marais
- Morning: Montmartre, Sacré-Cœur, and panoramic views.
- Afternoon: Le Marais for boutiques, Jewish quarter, and Musée Picasso.
- Evening: Local bistro and stroll through cobbled streets.

## 5-Day Paris Itinerary (Deeper exploration)

Day 1 — Louvre & Île de la Cité  
Day 2 — Eiffel Tower & Musée d'Orsay  
Day 3 — Versailles day trip or Château de Fontainebleau  
Day 4 — Le Marais, Centre Pompidou, and Canal Saint-Martin  
Day 5 — Montmartre, Père Lachaise Cemetery, and food markets (Marché d'Aligre)

Why 5 days: Adds a palace day trip and time to enjoy markets, galleries and neighborhoods.

## Variations: Family-Friendly vs. Young Explorer

For families
- Focus on Jardin des Tuileries, Cité des Enfants at Cité des Sciences, and boat rides.
- Stay centrally to reduce transit.

For young explorers
- Explore nightlife in Oberkampf, street art in Belleville, and late-night jazz in Saint-Germain.

## Short FAQ

Q: How long in Paris?  
A: 3 days for highlights; 5 days for a relaxed visit, museums, and a palace trip.

Q: Best way to book Eiffel Tower?  
A: Buy official timed tickets online to avoid long queues.

## Summary
Paris blends world-class museums with lived-in neighborhoods. Adapt the 3- or 5-day plans to your pace and book major attractions ahead to maximize sightseeing.
