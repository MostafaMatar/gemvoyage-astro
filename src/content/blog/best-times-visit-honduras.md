# Best Times to Visit Honduras — Ruins, Reefs & Rainforest

TL;DR: Dry season (Dec–Apr) is the most comfortable for diving on Bay Islands, exploring Copán ruins and rainforest hikes. The wet season (May–Nov) reduces visibility for diving and can make inland roads harder to navigate, but offers lush landscapes and lower prices.

## Why timing matters
Honduras’s Caribbean Bay Islands (Roatán, Utila) and inland archaeological sites require different planning: calm seas and clear waters for diving are found in dry months; seasonal rains can limit access to remote lodges and archaeological sites.

## Best months by interest
- Bay Islands (Roatán, Utila) diving: Dec–Apr for clear visibility and calm seas.  
- Copán Ruins: Dec–Apr for comfortable archaeology and fewer muddy trails.  
- Pico Bonito & La Mosquitia: Dec–Apr for better trail conditions and wildlife spotting.

## Regional quick notes
- Roatán: international flights and dive infrastructure—dry season is busiest.  
- Copán: UNESCO ruins best in shoulder months to avoid heavy rains and the worst heat.  
- La Mosquitia: remote and seasonal—book specialized operators for safe access.

## 5-day sample itinerary (family-friendly)
Day 1: Tegucigalpa arrival and transfer to Copán or fly to La Ceiba.  
Day 2: Explore Copán ruins—family-friendly tours and onsite museum.  
Day 3: Transfer to La Ceiba and ferry to Roatán — beach time and snorkel.  
Day 4: Diving or snorkeling day trip with PADI-certified operators.  
Day 5: Relax and return to Tegucigalpa for departure.

## Packing checklist
- Reef-safe sunscreen, insect repellent, quick-dry clothing and sturdy shoes for ruins and rainforest trails.

## Budget & booking tips
- Roatán and dive liveaboards fill in dry season—book early. Inland transport can require flexible timing and local guidance.

## Safety & family tips
- Use licensed dive operators and child-certified activities. In remote areas, travel with reputable guides and carry emergency supplies.

## Quick FAQ
Q: When is the best time for diving in Roatán?  
A: Dec–Apr provides the clearest water and calmest conditions.  
Q: Is Copán suitable for children?  
A: Yes—short trails and on-site facilities make it an accessible family visit.
