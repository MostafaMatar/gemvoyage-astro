# Best Times to Visit South Korea — Short Guide

TL;DR: Spring (late Mar–May) and autumn (Oct–Nov) are ideal—clear skies, mild temps and colorful landscapes. Winter (Dec–Feb) is perfect for skiing and ice festivals; summer brings humidity and monsoon rain in July.

## Why timing matters
South Korea’s four-season climate means festivals and activities are strongly seasonal. Cherry blossom timing is short but magical; autumn foliage is vivid across the peninsula. Avoid Chuseok (Korean Thanksgiving, Sep–Oct variability) if you don’t want closed businesses and busy transport.

## Best months by interest
- Cherry blossoms: late Mar–early Apr (southern areas earlier, northern later).
- Autumn leaves: Oct–Nov for crisp weather and vivid scenery.
- Skiing: Dec–Mar in Gangwon-do and higher altitudes.
- Beaches & islands (Jeju): Jun–Aug, though peak crowds in summer.

## Regional quick notes
- Seoul: spring and autumn best for city walks and palaces.
- Jeju Island: year-round appeal—best May–Jun and Sep for beaches and hiking.
- Gyeongju & Busan: autumn offers great coastal and cultural experiences.

## 5-day sample itinerary (family-friendly)
Day 1: Seoul — Gyeongbokgung, Insadong and kid-friendly museums.  
Day 2: Day trip to DMZ or Nami Island (kid-friendly nature).  
Day 3: Train to Busan — Haeundae beach and aquarium.  
Day 4: Ferry to Jeju — Hallasan hike (easy family trails) or beach.  
Day 5: Return to Seoul or extend for ski resort fun in winter.

## Packing checklist
- Spring/Autumn: layers, light waterproof jacket, comfortable shoes.
- Winter: warm insulated coat, gloves and thermal layers for mountain trips.
- Summer: breathable clothes, umbrella for monsoon showers.

## Budget & booking tips
- Golden Week-like travel spikes during Chuseok—book trains and flights early.
- Shoulder seasons provide cheaper hotels and clearer skies.

## Safety & family tips
- Public transport is excellent—kids love subway rides; follow local etiquette.
- Accommodation often child-friendly; Korean food has plenty of kid-friendly options.
- Track weather during winter for icy conditions and during summer for typhoon alerts.

## Quick FAQ
Q: When do cherry blossoms peak in Seoul?  
A: Typically late March to early April, but yearly variation is common.  
Q: Is South Korea good for families?  
A: Very—great public transport, safe cities and many educational attractions.
