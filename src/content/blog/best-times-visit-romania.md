# Best Times to Visit Romania — Castles, Mountains & Folklore

TL;DR: May–June and Sep–Oct are the sweet spots—mild weather, active festivals and clear mountain hikes. Summer is warm for coastal time on the Black Sea; winter caters to skiing in the Carpathians and festive markets.

## Why timing matters
Romania blends medieval towns, wild mountains and rural traditions that shine in certain months. Transylvania’s famous castles and medieval towns are most pleasant in spring and autumn; the mountains have short summer windows for high trails.

## Best months by interest
- Transylvania & castles (Bran, Brașov): May–June and Sep–Oct for light crowds and good weather.  
- Maramureș & rural traditions: summer and early autumn for festivals and craft markets.  
- Danube Delta wildlife: May–Jul for bird migrations and boat safaris.  
- Ski resorts (Poiana Brașov): Dec–Feb for snow sports.

## Regional quick notes
- Bucharest: spring and autumn for walks and cultural attractions.  
- Transylvanian villages: spring for blossoms; autumn for harvest festivals.  
- Danube Delta: bring insect repellent in warmer months.

## 5-day sample itinerary (family-friendly)
Day 1: Bucharest — Palace of Parliament overview and parks.  
Day 2: Travel to Brașov — city walls and cable car to Tampa.  
Day 3: Day trip to Bran Castle and Rasnov Fortress for kids.  
Day 4: Drive north to Maramureș for wooden churches and folk experiences.  
Day 5: Return via Sibiu or continue to the Danube Delta for nature.

## Packing checklist
- Layers for mountain variability, waterproof jacket for spring showers, comfortable shoes for cobbles and trails.

## Budget & booking tips
- Romania is value-friendly—book peak summer coastal and ski season stays in advance. Consider guesthouses for authentic and family-friendly stays.

## Safety & family tips
- Standard urban safety applies; rural roads can be narrow—allow extra travel time. Respect local traditions and modest dress at religious sites.

## Quick FAQ
Q: When is best to visit Bran Castle?  
A: Shoulder seasons avoid the biggest crowds while giving pleasant weather.  
Q: Is Romania good for wildlife watching?  
A: Yes—the Danube Delta and Carpathians offer excellent birding and larger-mammal chance.
