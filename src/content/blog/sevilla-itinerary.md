# Sevilla in 3 or 5 Days — Practical Itineraries and Travel Tips

Seville is the heart of Andalusian culture: grand palaces, orange-scented streets and passionate flamenco. Plan early starts in summer and book Alcázar and cathedral tickets in advance.

## 3-Day Sevilla Itinerary (Highlights)
Day 1 — Alcázar, Seville Cathedral and Giralda tower.  
Day 2 — Plaza de España, María Luisa Park, and a river stroll.  
Day 3 — Triana neighborhood, ceramic shops, and an evening flamenco show.

## 5-Day Sevilla Itinerary (Deeper exploration)
Add: day trip to Córdoba or Jerez, a tapas and sherry tasting route, and slower neighborhood exploration.

## Variations
For families — interactive museum visits and riverboat rides.  
For young explorers — nightlife in Alameda and late tapas tours.

## Short FAQ
Q: When to visit? A: Spring and autumn for best weather; July–August are very hot.  
Q: Is flamenco suitable for families? A: Yes — there are family-friendly shows.

## Summary
Seville pairs monumental sights with lively street life. Use 3 days for core highlights or 5 days for relaxed day trips and deeper cultural experiences.
