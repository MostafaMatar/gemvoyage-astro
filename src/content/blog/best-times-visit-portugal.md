# Best Times to Visit Portugal — Compact Guide

TL;DR: Spring (Apr–Jun) and autumn (Sep–Oct) are ideal for beaches, cities and wine regions—mild weather and fewer crowds. Summer is busy on the Algarve; winter is mild but some services slow.

## Why timing matters
Portugal's Atlantic coast has a long beach season but crowds spike in July–Aug. Lisbon and Porto are great year-round; the Douro wine harvest in Sept–Oct is a highlight for food and wine lovers.

## Best months by interest
- Lisbon & Porto city breaks: Apr–Jun, Sep–Oct.
- Algarve beaches: May–Jun and Sep for warm sea with fewer crowds.
- Douro Valley & wine harvest: Sep–Oct.
- Surfing (Nazare, Peniche): Sep–Nov and Mar–May.

## Regional quick notes
- Lisbon: manageable year-round; spring and autumn best for walking tours.
- Porto & Douro: harvest season for foodies; charming in winter with fewer tourists.
- Alentejo: spring wildflowers and autumn for rural escapes.

## 5-day sample itinerary (family-friendly)
Day 1: Lisbon — tram ride, Belém pastries and kid-friendly museums.  
Day 2: Day trip to Sintra — palaces and easy hikes.  
Day 3: Train to Porto — riverside stroll and river cruise.  
Day 4: Douro Valley day tour or easy family vineyard visit.  
Day 5: Algarve or coastal beach day before departure.

## Packing checklist
- Layers for coastal breeze, swimwear for summer, comfortable shoes for cobbled towns.

## Budget & booking tips
- Book Algarve stays early for summer; shoulder seasons combine value and good weather.
- Train travel is efficient—book intercity in advance for best prices.

## Safety & travel tips
- Portugal is family-friendly and generally safe—standard city precautions apply.
- Tap water is safe; coffee culture means plenty of family-friendly cafés.

## Quick FAQ
Q: When is the Douro wine harvest?  
A: Late Sep to Oct depending on the year.  
Q: Best time for Algarve without crowds?  
A: May–June or September.
