# Best Times to Visit Guatemala — Volcanoes, Lakes & Ruins

TL;DR: Dry season (Nov–Apr) is the best travel window—clear skies at Lake Atitlán, accessible hiking around Antigua and easy access to Tikal in the Petén lowlands. Rainy season (May–Oct) delivers lush landscapes and lower prices but can affect rural roads.

## Why timing matters
Guatemala’s highlands have cooler nights and tropical lowlands are wetter. The dry season provides the most predictable conditions for ancient ruins and volcano treks. Cultural events like Semana Santa in Antigua are major draws and require early bookings.

## Best months by interest
- Antigua: Nov–Apr for festivals, hiking and comfortable days.  
- Lake Atitlán: Dec–Apr for mirror-like lake mornings and village visits.  
- Tikal & Petén: Feb–Apr for dry, easier jungle exploration.  
- Highlands treks: Dec–Mar and Sep–Oct for clearer trails.

## Regional quick notes
- Antigua: famously atmospheric during Holy Week—book months ahead for that period.  
- Lake Atitlán: village-hopping, kayaking and family-friendly cultural stays—shoulder seasons quieter.  
- Tikal: early mornings for wildlife and sunrise temple views—dry season preferred.

## 5-day sample itinerary (family-friendly)
Day 1: Guatemala City arrival and transfer to Antigua—gentle city walk.  
Day 2: Antigua — volcano viewpoint or coffee farm visit with kids.  
Day 3: Travel to Lake Atitlán — boat between villages and cultural workshops.  
Day 4: Fly to Flores and visit Tikal—sunrise or morning exploration.  
Day 5: Return to Guatemala City via Flores or extend in the highlands.

## Packing checklist
- Layers for highland nights, lightweight tropical clothing for lowlands, insect repellent and good walking shoes.

## Budget & booking tips
- Dry season brings peak crowds—book lodges and local guides ahead. Rainy season offers lower prices but expect occasional transport delays.

## Safety & family tips
- Use reputable transport for night transfers; carry photocopies of documents. Stay aware of altitude changes when moving between highlands and lowlands.

## Quick FAQ
Q: When is Semana Santa in Antigua?  
A: It varies each year (March/April)—a spectacular cultural event but very busy.  
Q: Is Tikal good for kids?  
A: Yes—early-morning jungle encounters and short hikes are family-friendly with proper insect protection.
