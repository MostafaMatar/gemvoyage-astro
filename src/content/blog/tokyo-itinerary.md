# Tokyo in 3 or 5 Days — Practical Itineraries and Travel Tips

Tokyo is simultaneously futuristic and traditional: neon-lit intersections and capsule hotels sit alongside centuries-old temples. Efficient public transport means you can cover many neighborhoods quickly, but take time to savor local cuisine and quieter gardens.

Best months: March–May (cherry blossom), Sept–Nov (comfortable weather). Purchase a Suica card for easy transit.

## 3-Day Tokyo Itinerary (Highlights)

Day 1 — Shibuya, Harajuku & Shinjuku
- Morning: Shibuya Crossing, Hachiko statue, then Meiji Shrine in Yoyogi Park.
- Afternoon: Harajuku’s Takeshita Street for quirky fashion; Omotesando for cafe culture.
- Evening: Shinjuku night views from Metropolitan Building observation deck and dinner in Omoide Yokocho.

Day 2 — Asakusa & Ueno
- Morning: Senso-ji Temple in Asakusa and Nakamise shopping street.
- Afternoon: Ueno Park and museums (Tokyo National Museum or Ueno Zoo).
- Evening: Try izakaya dining in Ueno.

Day 3 — Modern Odaiba or Akihabara
- Morning and afternoon: Choose Odaiba for waterfront attractions and teamLabs (book ahead) or Akihabara for electronics and anime culture.
- Evening: River cruise on the Sumida or explore local nightlife.

## 5-Day Tokyo Itinerary (Deeper exploration)

Day 1 — Shibuya & Harajuku  
Day 2 — Asakusa & Ueno museums  
Day 3 — Akihabara & Kanda bookshops, Jimbocho  
Day 4 — Day trip: Nikko or Hakone (onsen views of Mount Fuji on clear days)  
Day 5 — Tsukiji outer market, Ginza shopping, and a luxurious dinner

Why 5 days: Includes day trips, specialized neighborhoods, and time for food markets and gardens.

## Variations: Family-Friendly vs. Young Explorer

For families
- Prioritize kid-friendly spots: Ueno Zoo, teamLabs Borderless, Sumida Aquarium, and kid-focused museums.
- Use taxis for shorter transfers with small children.

For young explorers
- Dive into late-night karaoke, themed cafés, thrift shopping in Shimokitazawa, and underground music spaces.

## Short FAQ

Q: Is Tokyo expensive?  
A: It varies — food and public transport can be affordable; high-end dining and hotels cost more.

Q: How to get around?  
A: Use JR lines, Tokyo Metro, and IC cards (Suica/Pasmo).

Q: Do I need cash?  
A: Cards are widely accepted, but small vendors and markets often prefer cash.

## Summary
Tokyo offers a thrilling blend of neighborhoods. A 3-day trip hits major districts, while 5 days let you add day trips and deep-dive into food culture. Tailor the pace for families or adventurous young travelers.
