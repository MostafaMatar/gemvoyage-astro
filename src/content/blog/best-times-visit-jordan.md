# Best Times to Visit Jordan — Petra, Desert & Dead Sea

TL;DR: Spring (Mar–May) and autumn (Sep–Nov) are ideal—pleasant daytime temps, clear skies and comfortable nights in Petra and Wadi Rum. Summers get very hot inland; winter is mild but can be chilly at higher elevations.

## Why timing matters
Jordan’s attractions span desert, ancient ruins and the Dead Sea. Heat and occasional winter rains affect comfort and access. Timed well, you’ll avoid the worst heat in the valley and still enjoy balmy evenings and clear skies for stargazing.

## Best months by interest
- Petra & Wadi Rum exploration: Mar–May and Sep–Nov for comfortable hiking and cooler nights.  
- Dead Sea & Amman: Mar–May and Sep–Nov; winter (Dec–Feb) is mild enough for city breaks but evenings are cool.  
- Birding & nature: Mar–Apr during migration windows.

## Regional quick notes
- Petra: early morning or late afternoon visits reduce heat; some canyon sections are shaded but midday can be hot in summer.  
- Wadi Rum: evenings can be chilly outside summer—pack a warm layer for desert camps.  
- Aqaba (Red Sea): year-round diving; best water temps Apr–Oct.

## 5-day sample itinerary (family-friendly)
Day 1: Arrive Amman — Citadel and relaxed Old Town stroll.  
Day 2: Jerash ruins and drive to Dead Sea for float time.  
Day 3: Early departure to Petra — full-day exploration, stay near the site.  
Day 4: Wadi Rum overnight camp — jeep tour and stargazing.  
Day 5: Aqaba beach time and return via Amman.

## Packing checklist
- Layers for temperature swings (desert nights/city days), sturdy walking shoes, sun protection, refillable water bottle, modest clothing for cultural sites.

## Budget & booking tips
- Shoulder seasons (spring, autumn) balance weather and prices. Book Petra tours and desert camps in advance during peak months. Domestic flights to Aqaba helpful for saving road time.

## Safety & family tips
- Respect local customs; modest dress around towns and religious sites. Stay hydrated; sun protection is essential. Use licensed guides for desert and mountain trips.

## Quick FAQ
Q: Can you visit Petra in one day?  
A: Yes—one long day covers highlights; two days lets families move at a relaxed pace.  
Q: Is Jordan expensive?  
A: It’s moderate—savings by using local eatery options and shared transfers.
