# Best Times to Visit Azerbaijan — Baku, Mountains & Caspian Coast

TL;DR: Spring (Apr–Jun) and early autumn (Sep–Oct) are prime — mild weather for Baku’s Old City, vineyard and mountain visits, and comfortable hiking in the Caucasus. Summer is hot on the coast; winter is cooler and quieter inland.

## Why timing matters
Azerbaijan’s climate varies from the Caspian coast to the Greater Caucasus. For walking Baku’s streets, visiting Gobustan and hiking nearby mountains, choose shoulder seasons for the best combination of weather and fewer crowds.

## Best months by interest
- Baku city and cultural tours: Apr–Jun and Sep–Oct for festivals and moderate temperatures.  
- Sheki & mountain villages: May–Sep for mountain walks and cooler elevations.  
- Caspian coast: Jun–Aug for beach days with warmer sea temperatures.

## Regional quick notes
- Baku: wind can be a factor—spring and autumn often comfortable for strolling the Flame Towers and waterfront.  
- Gobustan: best visited in dry weather for rock art and mud volcanoes.  
- Gabala & Sheki: good summer escapes for families with cooler highland air.

## 5-day sample itinerary (family-friendly)
Day 1: Baku — Old City, Boulevard and carpet museum.  
Day 2: Day trip to Gobustan and mud volcanoes.  
Day 3: Travel to Sheki — historic khan’s palace and local crafts.  
Day 4: Head to Gabala for easy mountain hikes and cable car views.  
Day 5: Return to Baku and relax before departure.

## Packing checklist
- Layers and a windbreaker for Baku, sturdy shoes for cobbles and trails, sun protection for coastal days.

## Budget & booking tips
- Azerbaijan is reasonably priced—book domestic transfers early in peak summer and mountain lodgings in advance for family rooms.

## Safety & family tips
- Use official taxis and public transport in cities; local guides improve cultural visits in rural areas. Respect local customs and dress modestly at religious sites.

## Quick FAQ
Q: When is Baku least windy?  
A: Wind varies—shoulder months often feel milder.  
Q: Are mountain areas family-friendly?  
A: Yes—Sheki and Gabala have gentle hikes and child-friendly activities.
