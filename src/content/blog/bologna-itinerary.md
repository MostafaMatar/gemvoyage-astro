# Bologna in 3 or 5 Days — Practical Itineraries and Travel Tips

Bologna is a culinary capital with porticoed streets, lively markets and a youthful atmosphere thanks to its university.

## 3-Day Bologna Itinerary (Highlights)
Day 1 — Piazza Maggiore, Basilica di San Petronio and the Two Towers.  
Day 2 — Quadrilatero market, cooking class or food tour, and Santo Stefano complex.  
Day 3 — Day trip to Modena (balsamic vinegar) or Parma.

## 5-Day Bologna Itinerary (Deeper exploration)
Add: Ferrari Museum in Maranello, regional food factories, and relaxed exploration of university neighborhoods.

## Variations
Families — hands-on food workshops and easy walks; young explorers — aperitivo scenes and late cafés.

## Short FAQ
Q: Is Bologna expensive?  
A: Moderate—food experiences can be premium but markets offer value.

## Summary
Bologna is ideal for food-focused travel. A 3-day trip highlights culinary and historic must-sees; 5 days add regional indulgence.
