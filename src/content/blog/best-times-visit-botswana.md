# Best Times to Visit Botswana — Delta Magic & Big Game

TL;DR: May–Oct (dry season) is the gold standard—water levels recede to concentrate wildlife, making for exceptional game viewing in the Okavango Delta and Chobe. Early dry season (May–Jun) offers lush vegetation and good birding.

## Why timing matters
Botswana’s reliance on seasonal flooding in the Okavango creates dramatic wildlife patterns. Lodges operate seasonally and many experiences—mokoro canoe rides, game drives and walking safaris—are best timed to water levels and dry-season visibility.

## Best months by interest
- Okavango Delta: Jun–Oct when water channels provide excellent boat and game viewing.  
- Chobe National Park: Jun–Oct for huge herds and river-based sightings.  
- Moremi and private concessions: May–Oct for top-notch wildlife experiences and photographic light.

## Regional quick notes
- Okavango: luxury and mobile camps operate seasonally—book early for family-friendly units.  
- Chobe: boat cruises on the Chobe River are spectacular in dry months when animals congregate on the banks.  
- Central Kalahari: June–Sep may offer predator viewing and special desert landscapes.

## 5-day sample itinerary (family-friendly)
Day 1: Arrival in Maun — short orientation and cultural experience.  
Day 2: Transfer to Okavango camp — boat rides and easy walks.  
Day 3: Full-day safari with mokoro and game drive options tailored for children.  
Day 4: Fly to Chobe — afternoon river cruise and evening game drive.  
Day 5: Morning safari and return via Kasane or Maun.

## Packing checklist
- Neutral safari clothing, sun protection, lightweight long sleeves for sun and insects, and binoculars. Warm layers for cool mornings/evenings in dry season.

## Budget & booking tips
- Botswana is premium-priced—book lodges many months ahead for the dry season. Family packages often include game activities—check age policies.

## Safety & family tips
- Follow ranger instructions on all game activities. Choose lodges that provide child-friendly briefings and non-strenuous activities for younger guests.

## Quick FAQ
Q: When is the best time for mokoro in the Okavango?  
A: Jun–Oct when water channels are navigable and wildlife is concentrated.  
Q: Is Botswana family-friendly?  
A: Yes—many camps offer family tents, activities for children and educational ranger programs.
