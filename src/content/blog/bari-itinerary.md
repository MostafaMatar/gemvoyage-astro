# Bari in 3 or 5 Days — Practical Itineraries and Travel Tips

Bari is the gateway to Puglia: an authentic port city with a walkable old town, lively seafood scene, and easy access to trulli villages and seaside towns.

## Quick tips
- Best months: May–Sept for warm weather and beaches.  
- Transport: Regional trains and buses are good for nearby towns.

## 3-Day Bari Itinerary (Highlights)
Day 1 — Bari Vecchia: Basilica of San Nicola, Castello Svevo, and seaside promenade.  
Day 2 — Pane & focaccia tasting, fish market, and walking the harbor.  
Day 3 — Day trip to Alberobello (trulli) or Polignano a Mare.

## 5-Day Bari Itinerary (Deeper exploration)
Add: Ostuni and Matera day trip, relaxed beach time, and local winery visits.

## Variations
Families — beach days and easy train trips; young explorers — coastal nightlife and local street food.

## Short FAQ
Q: Is Matera reachable from Bari?  
A: Yes—regular trains/buses connect Bari and Matera in about an hour.

## Summary
Bari is a practical base for Puglia’s highlights. Use 3 days for the city and a core day trip; 5 days for wider regional exploration.
