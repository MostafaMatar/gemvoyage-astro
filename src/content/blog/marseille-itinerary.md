# Marseille in 3 or 5 Days — Practical Itineraries and Travel Tips

Marseille is a vibrant port city with a raw, multicultural energy. From the Old Port and historic Le Panier quarter to dramatic Calanques national park, Marseille suits travelers looking for coastal scenery combined with urban culture.

## 3-Day Marseille Itinerary (Highlights)

Day 1 — Vieux-Port & Le Panier
- Morning: Stroll the Vieux-Port and fish market.
- Afternoon: Explore Le Panier’s lanes and street art.
- Evening: Bouillabaisse dinner at a local brasserie.

Day 2 — Notre-Dame de la Garde & museums
- Morning: Visit Notre‑Dame de la Garde for panoramic views.
- Afternoon: Musée des Civilisations de l'Europe et de la Méditerranée (MuCEM).
- Evening: Sunset by the harbor.

Day 3 — Calanques National Park
- Full day: Hike or boat the Calanques for turquoise coves and coastal cliffs.

## 5-Day Marseille Itinerary (Deeper exploration)

Day 1 — Old Port & Le Panier  
Day 2 — Garde and MuCEM  
Day 3 — Calanques day trip  
Day 4 — Day trip to Cassis and wine tasting  
Day 5 — Local markets, Cours Julien street art, and neighborhood cafes

Why 5 days: Adds time for Calanques and neighboring coastal towns.

## Variations: Family-Friendly vs. Young Explorer

For families
- Choose boat-excursions in Calanques, visit the aquarium, and easy urban walks.

For young explorers
- Discover Cours Julien’s nightlife, street art, and affordable beach spots.

## Short FAQ

Q: Can I visit the Calanques in one day?  
A: Yes — guided boat trips or hikes run from Marseille and Cassis.

Q: Is Marseille safe to explore?  
A: Use normal city precautions and stick to well-traveled areas and daytime visits for some neighborhoods.

## Summary
Marseille combines rugged coastal beauty with city life. A 3-day plan covers the essentials; 5 days add relaxed Calanques and coastal village visits.
