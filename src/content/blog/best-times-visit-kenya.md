# Best Times to Visit Kenya — Safaris & Coast

TL;DR: June–October is the prime dry season for safaris and the Great Wildebeest Migration crossings (Maasai Mara). January–February is an excellent alternative for calving and birding, while the coast is best Dec–Mar for beach time.

## Why timing matters
Kenya’s wildlife visibility and migration events are seasonal. Dry months concentrate animals at waterholes, making game viewing easier. Coastal weather differs—monsoon windows and sea conditions affect swimming and diving.

## Best months by interest
- Classic safaris & wildebeest river crossings: Jul–Oct (Maasai Mara peak).  
- Calving and birding: Jan–Mar in some reserves.  
- Coastal beaches (Mombasa, Diani): Dec–Mar for dry, sunny beach days.

## Regional quick notes
- Maasai Mara: plan July–October for river crossings; book well in advance.  
- Amboseli & Tsavo: year-round but drier months give best sightings.  
- Kenya coast: check currents and seasonal sea conditions for diving.

## 5-day sample itinerary (family-friendly)
Day 1: Nairobi — David Sheldrick Elephant Orphanage and Giraffe Centre.  
Day 2: Drive/fly to Maasai Mara — evening game drive.  
Day 3: Full-day game drives with picnic and cultural village visit.  
Day 4: Fly to Mombasa or Diani — beach time and snorkelling.  
Day 5: Relax and return via Nairobi.

## Packing checklist
- Neutral-colored clothing for safaris, sun protection, binoculars and closed-toe shoes. Malaria precautions in some regions—consult a clinic.

## Budget & booking tips
- Peak safari season requires early lodge and flight booking. Look for family-friendly packages and multi-night deals to stretch value.

## Safety & family tips
- Follow guide instructions on game drives; keep windows and doors closed near wildlife. Use registered operators for beach water activities.

## Quick FAQ
Q: When is the Great Wildebeest Migration in Kenya?  
A: River crossing peaks around Jul–Oct, but migrations are dynamic—check yearly predictions.  
Q: Is Kenya family-friendly?  
A: Yes—many lodges offer family rooms, child-focused game drives and easy wildlife encounters.
