# Venice in 3 or 5 Days — Practical Itineraries and Travel Tips

Venice is unique: narrow alleys, canals, and a fragile beauty that rewards early mornings and off-peak wandering.

## 3-Day Venice Itinerary (Highlights)
Day 1 — St. Mark’s Basilica, Doge’s Palace, and Campanile views.  
Day 2 — Rialto Market, Grand Canal vaporetto trip and hidden sestieri (neighborhoods).  
Day 3 — Murano, Burano and Torcello lagoon islands.

## 5-Day Venice Itinerary (Deeper exploration)
Add: Lido beach day, Jewish Ghetto tour, art biennale/exhibition visits and day trips to nearby Veneto towns.

## Variations
Families — boat rides and glass-blowing demonstrations; young explorers — late-night canal-side bars and local bacari (wine bars).

## Short FAQ
Q: Best time to visit Venice?  
A: Spring and autumn to avoid peak summer crowds; winter for quieter canals.

## Summary
Venice rewards slow exploration. 3 days cover essentials; 5 days let you savour islands, exhibitions and quieter corners.
