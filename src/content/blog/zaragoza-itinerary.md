# Zaragoza in 3 or 5 Days — Practical Itineraries and Travel Tips

Zaragoza offers Moorish palaces, Baroque basilicas and riverside charm, making it a quieter but rewarding Spanish city break.

## 3-Day Zaragoza Itinerary (Highlights)
Day 1 — Basilica del Pilar and Ebro river promenade.  
Day 2 — Aljafería Palace and the historic centre.  
Day 3 — Day trip to Monasterio de Piedra or nearby medieval villages.

## 5-Day Zaragoza Itinerary (Deeper exploration)
Add: Aragonese Pyrenees day trip, local tapas tours, and museum visits (Goya-related sites).

## Variations
For families — river parks and short guided tours.  
For young explorers — nightlife near El Tubo and outdoor activities in nearby mountains.

## Short FAQ
Q: How to reach Zaragoza? A: High-speed trains from Madrid and Barcelona make it an easy day trip.  
Q: Is Zaragoza busy? A: It’s less touristy than major Spanish cities, great for a relaxed pace.

## Summary
Zaragoza combines historical sites and natural day trips. A 3-day itinerary covers the core; 5 days allow Pyrenean escapes and cultural depth.
